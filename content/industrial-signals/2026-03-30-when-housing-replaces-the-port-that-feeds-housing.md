# When Housing Replaces the Port That Feeds Housing

NSW’s Bays West promises up to 8,500 homes on Glebe Island.  
It will also end bulk cement and gypsum handling there by 2030.

This is not just a housing story.  
It is a logistics story.

When housing grows by displacing the infrastructure that feeds construction, the bottleneck does not disappear.  
It moves.

**Source:** NSW Government Bays West Place Strategy (Glebe Island)
